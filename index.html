01 — About

The teacher who codes.
The developer who teaches.

I stand at the intersection of two crafts most people keep separate: teaching people how computers think, and building the software that makes them think faster.

For the past several years I've taught Informatics in Turkistan, Kazakhstan — turning abstract concepts like algorithms, logic and data structures into lessons students actually look forward to. Outside the classroom, I write production code: web applications, AI‑assisted tools, embedded robotics and learning platforms that put the same ideas I teach into practice.

I believe the best educators are also builders. Every tool I ship — from an LMS for chemistry students to a colour‑sorting robot built with Arduino — is a way of testing whether the ideas I teach actually hold up in the real world.
